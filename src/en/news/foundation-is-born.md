We are officially introducing **Fundación Martina**, a non-profit organisation created to
fund biomedical and genetic research into ataxias and rare diseases linked to the VPS13D
gene. Based in A Coruña, Galicia, our goal is to support affected patients and families,
and to drive forward lines of research that today receive scarcely any resources.

The Foundation takes its name from Martina, a ten-year-old girl living with a
neurodegenerative ataxia. Her story, and that of the many families facing a similar
situation, is the starting point of a project that aims to turn uncertainty into
knowledge, and isolation into companionship.

![Fundación Martina logo](/assets/img/logo-hero.webp)

Since its founding, the Foundation has set out three lines of work that sum up our
purpose: **researching**, by funding and promoting scientific projects on ataxias and
rare diseases; **supporting**, by accompanying patients and families with information
and guidance; and **transforming**, by sharing science with rigour and raising public
awareness of these low-prevalence conditions.

In the coming weeks we will be announcing the Foundation's first steps: the launch of
the Scientific Advisory Committee, our first collaboration agreements with research
centres, and the inaugural event where we will introduce the project to everyone who
wants to join us.

Thank you to everyone who is already part of this journey.
